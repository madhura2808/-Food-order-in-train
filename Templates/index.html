TrainNames

Rajdhani Express

The Rajdhani Express is a series of passenger train services in India operated by Indian Railways connecting the national capital New Delhi with the capitals or the largest cities of various states. Rajdhani, derived from the Sanskrit means "capital". Introduced in 1969, the trains are amongst the premium services operated by Indian Railways and regularly get a higher priority on the railway network.

Mahalaxmi Express

Mahalaxmi Express is the train belonging to South Central Indian Railways. The train is connecting Mumbai CSMT and Kolhapur SCSMT in India. The train service frequency is daily, and is supposedly covering a total distance of 518 km in about 10 hours and 57 minutes. There are 22 coaches available in the express, and it includes AC first class, AC second class, AC third class, sleeper and general unreserved category.

HariPriya Express

Haripriya Express Train Number 17415 from Tirupati Main to Kolhapur CSMT is a Mail/Express train belonging to Indian Railways in SCR/South Central Zone. It has started services from Wed Sep 23, 1998. It has 30 halts. 127 intermediate Stations between Tirupati Main and Kolhapur CSMT Covering a total distance of 903 km in 19h 35m time.

Tirupati Express

The Chennai Central–Tirupati Express is an Express train belonging to Southern Railway zone that runs between Chennai Central and Tirupati in India. It is currently being operated with 16053/16054 train numbers on a daily basis.The 16053/Chennai Central–Tirupati Express has an average speed of 43 km/h and covers 147 km in 3h 25m. The 16054/Tirupati–Chennai Central Express has an average speed of 40 km/h and covers 147 km in 3h 40m.
